Activities & Achievements

Activities can help children develop confidence, discipline, creativity, and friendship. The examples below show suitable programmes that visitors and community groups may support.

Education Support

Homework guidance, reading circles, motivational talks, and school supply drives help children stay engaged with learning.

Life Skills

Gardening, cleaning routines, cooking assistance, and teamwork activities build independence and practical confidence.

Community Events

Festive visits, sports days, charity meals, and volunteer programmes strengthen bonds between the home and society.

Possible Achievements

  • Improved school readiness through donated stationery and reading support.
  • Stronger community awareness through visits from local organizations.
  • Better daily comfort through food, hygiene, and clothing contributions.
  • More positive social development through group activities and mentoring.

Volunteer Reminder

Volunteers should request permission before organizing activities and should avoid taking or sharing children's photos without proper consent.

Contact the home before planning a visit.
